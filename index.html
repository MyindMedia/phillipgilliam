A John Talion Mystery · Book Two

At All Times

"Lust, like loyalty, can be deadly."

Crime · Noir Thriller

Ex-homicide detective John Talion finds the battered, knee-capped body of his childhood friend — ex-boxer Yaqui Galindez — dumped outside his office. The murder is brutal, deliberate, and personal, left with a photograph of a priceless synthetic diamond, the Estrella de la Muerte, and a plea for help.

Pulled back into a city that no longer wants him, Talion follows a violent web linking a tortured gemologist, a fixed championship fight, dirty cops, and a young mother whose secret life drew her too close to the wolves. As bodies accumulate, the message sharpens: he is no longer investigating the conspiracy — he's part of it.

Black Leather Apron

"A suffocating tale of murder, greed, lust, revenge, and insanity."

Crime · Mystery · Thriller

Ex-homicide detective John Talion is hired to investigate the brutal murder of Sharon Bowling, daughter of a prominent Baltimore family. What begins as a private case unravels into a string of killings with identical signatures, cryptic messages at every scene, and a chilling thread that stretches all the way back to Whitechapel — and Jack the Ripper himself.

Talion's investigation drags him through the city's old money, its quiet corruption, and the mind of a killer who believes in the principle of retributive justice. By the time the pattern snaps into focus, the only question left is whether Talion can survive recognizing it.

The Author

Phillip Gilliam.

Phillip Gilliam was born in Norfolk, Virginia, and earned a Bachelor of Science in Physics and Engineering from Morgan State University in 1981. He spent four decades as a communications engineer at Verizon, earning two U.S. patents along the way — 5,957,878 for custom biomechanics compression garments, and 7,803,092 for an exercise device he calls the Sisyphean Chamber.

Gilliam fell for film noir young — Bogart, Cagney, Brando — and the records that played underneath those films: Sinatra, Ellington, Billie Holiday. You can hear all of it in the cadence of the John Talion Mysteries.

Black Leather Apron was born from a research trip to Louie's Book Store, where Gilliam went chasing Jack the Ripper and came back with a novel. He walked the alleys of Baltimore's Fells Point at night with a camera, mapping them onto the 1888 Whitechapel crimes, until the city itself became a character.

At All Times continues the series, and a third Talion novel — Vengeance Is Mine — is in the works. A fourth story, centered on an inventor called The Sisyphean Chamber, is planned beyond the trilogy.
