A John Talion Mystery · Book Two

At All Times

"Lust, like loyalty, can be deadly."

Crime · Noir Thriller

Ex-homicide detective John Talion walks out of his office and finds a body. It's his childhood friend, ex-boxer Yaqui Galindez, beaten and knee-capped. Whoever did it left a photograph of a priceless synthetic diamond called the Estrella de la Muerte, and a plea for help.

Talion gets pulled back into a city that no longer wants him. The trail runs through a tortured gemologist, a fixed championship fight, dirty cops, and a young mother whose secret life drew her too close to the wolves. As bodies pile up, the message sharpens. He isn't investigating the conspiracy. He's part of it.

Black Leather Apron

"A suffocating tale of murder, greed, lust, revenge, and insanity."

Crime · Mystery · Thriller

Ex-homicide detective John Talion is hired to investigate the brutal murder of Sharon Bowling, daughter of a prominent Baltimore family. What begins as a private case unravels into a string of killings with identical signatures, cryptic messages at every scene, and a chilling thread that stretches all the way back to Whitechapel, and Jack the Ripper himself.

Talion's investigation drags him through the city's old money, its quiet corruption, and the mind of a killer who believes in the principle of retributive justice. By the time the pattern snaps into focus, the only question left is whether Talion can survive recognizing it.

About Phillip

Phillip Gilliam.

Phillip Gilliam was born in Norfolk, Virginia. He earned a Bachelor of Science in Physics and Engineering from Morgan State University in 1981, then spent the next four decades as a communications engineer at Verizon. Along the way he picked up two U.S. patents: 5,957,878 for custom biomechanics compression garments, and 7,803,092 for an exercise device he calls the Sisyphean Chamber.

He fell hard for film noir as a kid. Bogart, Cagney, Brando, and the records that played underneath those films: Sinatra, Ellington, Billie Holiday. You can hear all of that in the rhythm of the John Talion Mysteries.

Black Leather Apron started with a research trip to Louie's Book Store. Gilliam went looking for Jack the Ripper and came back with a novel. He walked the alleys of Baltimore's Fells Point at night with a camera, laying them over the 1888 Whitechapel crimes, until the city itself became a character.

At All Times continues the series. A third Talion novel, Vengeance Is Mine, is on the way. After that, a fourth story is planned around an inventor called The Sisyphean Chamber.
